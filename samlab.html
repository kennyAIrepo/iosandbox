🎯 SAM LAB · prompt → track → judge

You are validating SAM 2.1-small as our tap-to-lock + court + referee-exclusion engine. Your taps are the prompts; SAM locks a pixel-mask on that exact person/region and follows it through the clip. You judge whether the lock HOLDS.

How to run a session

  1. Footage: click 🎬 NBA sample (instant) — or 🖥 share the tab playing any game footage — or load a file ↓
  2. File/share: get to the action, then Grab clip (8s)
  3. Pick a role → tap that person on the big canvas:
    🏀 Player — tap 1–2 players you want locked
    🦓 Ref — tap EACH referee once (this is the ref fix!)
    🟦 Court — tap an empty patch of floor
  4. Propagate — SAM tracks every tapped object through all frames
  5. Play and scrub. Judge it (next section), file the verdict.

What to judge (the keypoints)

  1. Lock persistence: does a tapped player's mask survive screens, crossings, occlusions — without jumping to a different person? (This replaces tap-to-lock.)
  2. Ref exclusion: once tapped, does each ref stay masked the whole clip? 3 taps/game = refs solved at runtime, no training.
  3. Court edges: does the floor mask hug the real boundary (baseline, sidelines)? (This replaces the color-blob heuristic.)
  4. Mask quality on smalls: distant players — usable silhouettes or mush? (Decides small vs base+.)
